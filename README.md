# REVAN — A Fan-Written Television Series

> *"The black looks good on you."*

A feature-length, multi-season fan screenplay set in the Star Wars Legends universe, reimagining the story of Revan from the ground up, beginning not with a Jedi who fell, but a Sith Lord who chose.

---

## Overview

This is an original fan-written television series based on characters and lore from *Star Wars: Knights of the Old Republic* (BioWare, 2003) and *Star Wars: Knights of the Old Republic II — The Sith Lords* (Obsidian Entertainment, 2004), as well as broader Star Wars Legends continuity.

Format: Multi-season prestige television
Episodes: 10 per season
Rating: TV-MA / 18+
Themes: Moral collapse, moral ambiguity, faith vs. indoctrination, religion vs. morality, betrayal, friendship, found family

---

## Premise

The Mandalorian Wars did not end cleanly. A general named Revan, armed with incomplete information and no good options, made a decision that ended the war and damned him for it.

This is not a story about a Jedi who fell to the dark side.

This is a story about what happens when the institutions that are supposed to protect people fail them, and the man left holding the consequences.

Two key changes from the source material drive the entire series:

- Bastila Shan is a Jedi Master, not a Padawan. She has a student of her own. The contrast between how she and Revan teach — how they fight — reveals more about who they are than any confrontation between them.
- Revan chooses Vitiate. He is not brainwashed. He is not broken. He makes a deal, with full awareness, for reasons that will not be understood until Season 3.

Everything else follows from those two decisions.

## Characters

### Returning Characters — KOTOR & Legends

Revan — Sith Lord. Revolutionary. Warlord. A man the Force bends around, who cannot save a child, who lays down his arms for a woman he loves, who makes a deal with the most dangerous being in the galaxy because he believes it leads somewhere better. The audience decides what he is.

Bastila Shan — Jedi Master. Pragmatic, principled, and burdened with a student she is responsible for. Her fall is not seduction. It is pressure applied to someone who was never given room to be uncertain.

Malak — Not an apprentice. A friend. Loyal to a fault. Came up with Revan, believed in Revan, and betrayed Revan — which makes it the worst kind of betrayal. A secondary leader, not a shadow.

Meetra Surik — Stays. Builds a life as an exile alongside Revan and Bastila. Finds something close to happiness in it. One word brings Revan back from the edge on Tatooine.

Darth Traya (Kreia) — The most dangerous person in the series. You'll have to see the rest later xD

Darth Sion — You'll have to see later xD

Darth Nihilus — What the mass shadow generator left behind. Haunts Revan as a living consequence.

Vitiate — You'll have to see later xD

The Aing-Tii Monks *(Legends, reimagined)* — Force-neutral artifact traders. A minor but philosophically significant presence in the world.

---

### Original Characters

Ekhralon — Revan's apprentice. Her loyalty is not blind, it is chosen, which makes it more complicated. The philosophical next generation of Exiles.

Serave - Bastila's Padawan. Carries the arc of idealism meeting reality that Bastila herself no longer can. Sees everything.

Zeravun - The Korribani Politician. Genuine friendship with Revan. He's carrying secrets, that when revealed change the balance of power for the entire galaxy.

## A Note on Lore

This series expands on established Legends continuity in several places. Those expansions are intentional and will not be spoiled here. The tragedy of the Mandalorian Wars is not fully understood until Traya arrives. The reason for Revan's deal with Vitiate is not fully understood until Season 3.

Some things are better discovered in order.
