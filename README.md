# REVAN — A Fan-Written Television Series

> *"The black looks good on you."*

A feature-length, multi-season fan screenplay/visual screenplay set in the Star Wars Legends universe. Centered around Darth Revan, a Jedi turned Sith lord who finds death, destruction, friendship, and love in his endeavour to make the galaxy a better place.

---

## Overview

This is an original fan-written television series based on characters and lore from *Star Wars: Knights of the Old Republic* (BioWare, 2003) and *Star Wars: Knights of the Old Republic II — The Sith Lords* (Obsidian Entertainment, 2004), as well as broader Star Wars Legends stories. This expands on the original works of KOTOR and KOTOR 2, and adds in characters never before seen in KOTOR but exist in the wider extended universe.

Format: Multi-season prestige television
Episodes: 10 per season
Rating: TV-MA / 18+
Themes: Moral collapse, moral ambiguity, faith vs. indoctrination, religion vs. morality, betrayal, friendship, found family, tragicomedy

---

## Premise

The Mandalorian Wars did not end happily. General Revan, leading the Revanites (a faction of the Jedi that separates from the order to help the republic fight the Mandalorian threat), ends the war in apotheosis. For the next 10 years he fights the Jedi as a Sith lord hell bent on overthrowing and re-establishing a new, better order. 

This is not a story about a Jedi who fell to the dark side, this is a story about what happens when the institutions that are supposed to protect people fail them, and how they deal with the consequences of their actions long term.

Two key changes from the source material drive the entire series from the beginning:

- Bastila Shan is a Jedi Master, not a Padawan. She has a student of her own. The contrast between how she and Revan teach, how they fight, how they deal with pain and consequence, reveals more about who they are than any confrontation between them.
- Revan chooses Vitiate. He is not brainwashed, he makes a deal, with full awareness of who he is and what he represents, for reasons that will not be understood until Season 3 of the show.

## Characters

### Returning Characters — KOTOR & Legends

Revan — Sith Lord, revolutionary and warlord. A man the Force bends around, who cannot save a child, who lays down his arms for a woman he loves, who makes a deal with the most dangerous being in the galaxy because he believes it leads somewhere better. The audience decides what he is. <- mimics the choice made in the game as to whether or not to go dark or light.

Bastila Shan — Jedi Master, she's pragmatic, principled, and burdened with a student she is responsible for. Her fall is not seduction, it is pressure applied to someone who was never given room to be uncertain of the Jedi council.

Malak — Not an apprentice, a friend, loyal to a fault. Came up with Revan, believed in Revan, and betrayed Revan, which makes it the worst kind of betrayal. A secondary leader, shadowing and learning Revan's control structure.

Meetra Surik — Spy in the outer rim with corporate connections. Loyal to Revan through everything.

Darth Traya (Kreia) — The most dangerous person in the series. You'll have to see the rest later xD

Darth Sion — You'll have to see later xD

Darth Nihilus — What the mass shadow generator left behind, he haunts Revan and is his consequence.

Vitiate — You'll have to see later xD

The Aing-Tii Monks *(Legends, reimagined)* — Force-neutral artifact traders, a minor but philosophically significant presence in the world.

---

### Original Characters

Ekhralon — Revan's apprentice. Her loyalty is not blind, it is chosen due to tragedy. The philosophical next generation of Exiles.

Serave - Bastila's Padawan. Carries the idealism that Bastila herself loses. Sees everything that happens and still can't shake the morality behind it all.

Zeravun - The Korribani Politician. Genuine friendship with Revan, he's carrying secrets, that when revealed change the balance of power for the entire galaxy.

## A Note on Lore

This series expands on established Legends continuity in several places. Those expansions are intentional and will not be spoiled here. The tragedy of the Mandalorian Wars is not fully understood until Traya arrives. The reason for Revan's deal with Vitiate is not fully understood until Season 3.

Some things are better discovered in order of the story.
